Baku - The Windy City

Baku, the capital of Azerbaijan, is a city of stunning contrasts where the ancient East meets the modern West. Located on the Absheron Peninsula, its history is deeply rooted in the oil industry, which has funded its magnificent transformation. The city's core is the UNESCO World Heritage site, Icherisheher (Old City), a fortified complex containing the iconic Maiden Tower and the Palace of the Shirvanshahs...

Karabakh - The Cultural Cradle

Karabakh is the historical and cultural heartland of Azerbaijan, a land of breathtaking mountains, lush forests, and deep artistic heritage. For centuries, it has been revered as the cradle of Azerbaijani music, particularly Mugham, and the birthplace of countless poets, writers, and artists. The city of Shusha, often called the "pearl of Karabakh," is its cultural epicenter...

Quba - The Mountain Jewel

Located on the northeastern slopes of the Caucasus Mountains, Quba is a region renowned for its stunning natural beauty, verdant apple orchards, and rich tapestry of cultures. It serves as a perfect escape from the city bustle, offering cool mountain air and spectacular landscapes. One of its most famous attractions is the ancient village of Khinalig (Xınalıq)...

Naftalan - The Healing Oil

Naftalan is a globally unique resort town, famous for its one-of-a-kind natural resource: a special grade of crude oil with therapeutic properties. This oil is not used for fuel but for medicinal baths. For over a century, people from all over the world have traveled to Naftalan to seek treatment for a wide range of conditions...
