Baku - The Windy City

Baku, the capital of Azerbaijan, is a city of stunning contrasts where the ancient East meets the modern West. Located on the Absheron Peninsula, its history is deeply rooted in the oil industry, which has funded its magnificent transformation. The city's core is the UNESCO World Heritage site, Icherisheher (Old City), a fortified complex containing the iconic Maiden Tower and the Palace of the Shirvanshahs. This historical heart is encircled by a modern metropolis boasting architectural wonders like the Flame Towers and the fluid Heydar Aliyev Center.

Karabakh - The Cultural Cradle

Karabakh is the historical and cultural heartland of Azerbaijan, a land of breathtaking mountains, lush forests, and deep artistic heritage. For centuries, it has been revered as the cradle of Azerbaijani music, particularly Mugham, and the birthplace of countless poets, writers, and artists. The city of Shusha, often called the "pearl of Karabakh," is its cultural epicenter, known for its historic fortresses, mosques, and the famous Jidir Duzu plain.

Quba - The Mountain Jewel

Located on the northeastern slopes of the Caucasus Mountains, Quba is a region renowned for its stunning natural beauty, verdant apple orchards, and rich tapestry of cultures. It serves as a perfect escape from the city bustle. A highlight is the ancient village of Khinalig, one of Europe's highest settlements. The region is also home to the dramatic Afurja waterfall and the unique "Red Town" (Qırmızı Qəsəbə).

Naftalan - The Healing Oil

Naftalan is a globally unique resort town, famous for its one-of-a-kind natural resource: a special grade of crude oil with therapeutic properties. This oil is not used for fuel but for medicinal baths. For over a century, people have traveled to Naftalan to seek treatment for a wide range of conditions. The town is built around this industry, featuring numerous modern sanatoriums and wellness centers.

Sheki - The Silk Road Pearl

Sheki is one of Azerbaijan’s most picturesque and historic cities, a former stop on the legendary Silk Road. Its crown jewel is the 18th-century Palace of the Sheki Khans, world-famous for its 'shebeke' stained-glass windows. Another landmark is the Sheki Karvansaray, a preserved inn where merchants rested. The city is also a center of craftsmanship and unique cuisine, including 'piti' and 'Sheki halva'.

Ganja - The City of Nizami

Ganja is Azerbaijan's second-largest city and a place of immense historical importance. It is the birthplace of the great 12th-century poet Nizami Ganjavi, whose mausoleum is a major landmark. Key sites include the Juma Mosque, the Chokak Hamam (ancient bathhouse), and the peculiar "Bottle House," a residence decorated with nearly 50,000 glass bottles. The city also boasts large, beautiful parks, making it a pleasant destination.

Lankaran - The Citrus Paradise

Lankaran is a subtropical paradise on the Caspian coast. Its humid climate makes it famous for vast tea plantations, citrus groves, and rice paddies. Lankaran tea is a symbol of Azerbaijani hospitality. The city has several historical sites, including the old Lankaran Fortress. It is also a gateway to the Hirkan National Park, a protected forest with unique flora and fauna, including the Caucasian leopard.

Qabala - The Land of Legends

Gabala (Qəbələ) is a vibrant tourist hub blending ancient history with modern leisure. For over 600 years, it was the capital of ancient Caucasian Albania. Surrounded by the Caucasus Mountains, Gabala is a destination for all seasons. It features the Tufandag Mountain Resort for skiing, the serene Nohur Lake, the Gabaland amusement park, and a world-famous international music festival.

Goygol - The Blue Lake

Goygol, meaning "Blue Lake," is arguably Azerbaijan's most beautiful lake. Formed by an earthquake in 1139, it is a high-altitude lake of stunning, crystal-clear blue water, surrounded by dense forests within the Goygol National Park. The park has a rich biodiversity, and its long-preserved beauty makes it an unforgettable encounter with nature. Nearby, one can also find other beautiful lakes like Maralgol (Deer Lake).
